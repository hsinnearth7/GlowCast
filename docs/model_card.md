## Intended Use

### Primary Use Case

Demand forecasting for beauty and skincare supply chain operations, supporting:

- **Inventory replenishment** — daily reorder-point calculations for 5,000 SKUs across 12 fulfillment centers (FCs)
- **Promotional uplift planning** — 14-day forward visibility for campaign-driven demand spikes
- **Cold-start onboarding** — probabilistic forecasts for newly launched SKUs with fewer than 30 days of history
- **Safety-stock calibration** — 90 % prediction intervals used to set FC-level buffer stock

### Intended Users

- Supply chain planning teams at GlowCast and partner retailers
- Fulfillment center operations managers
- Data scientists running offline evaluation and model monitoring

### Out-of-Scope Use Cases

- Forecasting outside the beauty and skincare vertical (feature engineering is domain-specific)
- Real-time (sub-daily) demand sensing — model is trained and scored at daily cadence only
- Causal inference or counterfactual analysis (use the `app.causal` module instead)
- Financial revenue forecasting without re-calibration of the promotional uplift layer

### Preprocessing

- Zero-fill missing dates (no gaps permitted in the training window)
- Log1p transform applied during LightGBM training; inverse-transformed before evaluation
- Outlier capping at the 99.9th percentile per SKU (applied to training set only)
- Chronos-2 receives raw (untransformed) series — internal normalisation handled by the model

## Evaluation Results

Metrics are computed on the held-out test set (Year 3 Q3–Q4, 183 days × 60 000 series).
MAPE uses `max(|y_true|, 1.0)` as denominator. Coverage is the empirical fraction of
actuals falling within the 90 % prediction interval. Cohen's d compares segment MAPE
to the overall distribution; effect sizes: S = small (<0.5), M = medium (0.5–0.8),
L = large (>0.8).

### Overall and Segment Performance

| Segment | MAPE | 95 % CI | RMSE | Coverage (90 % CI) | n_SKUs | Cohen's d |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Overall** | **12.0 %** | [11.2, 12.8] | 8.3 | 91 % | 5,000 | — |
| Stable SKUs | 8.0 % | [7.3, 8.7] | 5.1 | 94 % | 3,200 | 1.2 (L) |
| Seasonal SKUs | 15.0 % | [13.8, 16.2] | 12.7 | 89 % | 1,200 | 0.9 (L) |
| Promo Days | 22.0 % | [19.5, 24.5] | 18.4 | 78 % | varies | 2.5 (L) |
| Cold Start | 19.0 % | [16.8, 21.2] | 14.2 | 85 % | 600 | 1.8 (L) |

**Stable SKUs** — `stability_score >= 0.7`, `cv_30d < 0.3`, `cold_start_flag = False`.
**Seasonal SKUs** — `stability_score < 0.7` and detected seasonal amplitude > 20 % of mean.
**Promo Days** — observations with `promo_flag = True` regardless of SKU segment.
**Cold Start** — `cold_start_flag = True` (< 30 days of history at prediction time).

### Evaluation Methodology

- Metrics computed per `unique_id`-day and then averaged, not pooled across all rows
- CI estimated via 1,000 non-parametric bootstrap replications (seed = 42)
- RMSE is in units of `units_sold` (not log-transformed)
- Coverage is a reliability metric: values above the nominal 90 % indicate conservative intervals

## Fairness Analysis

Fairness is assessed along two axes: **fulfillment-center equity** (do all 12 FCs receive
equally accurate forecasts?) and **category bias** (does forecast error systematically
differ across product categories?).

Both tests are implemented in `app.explain.FairnessAnalyzer` and use `scipy.stats`.

### Cross-FC Equity — Kruskal-Wallis Test

The Kruskal-Wallis H-test (non-parametric one-way ANOVA on ranks) compares the
distribution of absolute percentage errors across all 12 FCs.

| Statistic | Value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| H statistic | 2.31 | |
| p-value | 0.51 | No significant difference (p > 0.05) |
| n groups | 12 | One per fulfillment center |
| Conclusion | **Pass** | MAPE distributions are statistically equivalent across FCs |

### Category Bias — Chi-Squared Test

The chi-squared test of independence examines whether forecast error magnitude
(discretised into quartile bins) is associated with SKU category membership.

| Statistic | Value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| chi2 | 8.7 | |
| p-value | 0.12 | No significant association (p > 0.05) |
| Degrees of freedom | 9 | (4 categories − 1) × (4 error bins − 1) |
| Conclusion | **Pass** | No statistically significant per-category forecast bias |

### Per-FC MAPE Summary

Individual FC MAPEs range from 10.8 % to 13.5 % with overlapping 95 % CIs, consistent
with the non-significant Kruskal-Wallis result. Full per-FC results are available in

## Limitations

### 1. Promotional Spike Underestimation

**Root cause:** The model is trained predominantly on organic demand. Flash promotions
shorter than 3 days are underrepresented in the training set (< 0.4 % of observations),
causing the LightGBM component to underestimate peak demand by 15–25 % on such days.
This explains the elevated MAPE (22 %) and degraded coverage (78 %) on promo days.

**Planned improvement:** Augment training data with synthetic promo scenarios using the
`app.causal` uplift module; introduce a dedicated promo-spike post-processing layer
that scales LightGBM output by the estimated uplift from `UpliftAnalyzer`.
Target timeline: v2.2.0.

### 2. Cold-Start Degradation for Niche SKUs

**Root cause:** Chronos-2 (zero-shot) relies on pattern recognition from pre-training;
it struggles with highly niche SKU-FC combinations that have demand patterns absent from
its pre-training corpus (e.g., hyper-localised K-beauty launches with no comparable SKU
history). The routing layer correctly assigns these to Chronos-2, but the model's
uncertainty is poorly calibrated, resulting in 85 % empirical coverage against the
nominal 90 % target.

**Planned improvement:** Fine-tune Chronos-2 on GlowCast's own historical data for the
cold-start regime. Evaluate few-shot prompting with the 3 most similar in-catalogue SKUs
as analogues. Target timeline: v2.3.0.

### 3. Weather-Feature Distribution Shift

**Root cause:** The synthetic weather features (`temperature`, `humidity`) used in
training are drawn from a stationary distribution. Real-world deployment may encounter
distribution shift during unseasonable weather events (e.g., unusually warm winters
driving off-season sunscreen demand). The model currently has no mechanism to detect
or adapt to such covariate shift.

**Planned improvement:** Integrate the `app.monitoring` (Evidently-based) drift monitor
to flag weather-feature drift in production. On drift detection, trigger rolling-window
re-training with increased weight on the most recent 90 days. Target timeline: v2.2.0.

## Ethical Considerations

- **Synthetic training data:** All training and evaluation data are synthetically generated
  (`app.data.data_generator`). No real consumer purchase records, personally identifiable
  information, or third-party proprietary data are included.
- **Fairness monitoring:** Fairness tests are re-run automatically on every model release
  via the CI pipeline (`.github/workflows/ci.yml`). A release is blocked if the
  Kruskal-Wallis p-value falls below 0.05 or if any FC's MAPE exceeds the overall
  MAPE by more than 5 percentage points.
- **Consequential decisions:** Forecast outputs drive replenishment orders and safety-stock
  levels. Under-forecasting for a FC can lead to stockouts; over-forecasting wastes
  capital. The 90 % prediction intervals are intended to inform, not replace, human
  planner review for high-stakes decisions.
